Embryo Analysis

by Dias L A N Y (IT21146206)

Data Science
Sri Lanka Institute of Information Technology

Intoduction

Embryogenic callus identification is vital in coconut tissue culture, affecting the success rate of multiplication and regeneration.


Research Problem

Differentiating embryogenic from non-embryogenic callus manually is inefficient, reducing the effectiveness of tissue culture processes.


Research Gap

Coconut tissue culture embryos require precise monitoring to assess their viability and development at different stages. Currently, there is limited research on automated embryo analysis in coconut tissue culture, with most studies relying on manual inspection or generalized plant tissue culture models. Machine learning techniques could fill this gap by developing algorithms to analyze high-resolution images and detect subtle changes in embryo morphology and color, which are indicative of viability and developmental progression. Such an approach would enable early identification of non-viable embryos or developmental anomalies, improving overall success rates in coconut tissue culture.
